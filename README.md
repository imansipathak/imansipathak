## Who I Am

I'm an **AI Product Manager** who builds at the intersection of **large language models**, **enterprise compliance**, and **regulated financial services**. I believe AI's most important role in the next decade isn't writing code or generating images — it's helping organizations navigate regulatory complexity at a scale humans simply can't.

My work lives in the unglamorous but critical layer between "AI can do this" and "an OCC examiner would allow this." I design systems where AI accelerates governance, reduces risk exposure, and gives compliance teams superpowers — not shortcuts that create liability.

```
What I obsess over:
  ├── How LLMs reason about regulatory intent (not just keyword matching)
  ├── Approval workflows that enforce separation of duties at scale
  ├── AI-generated content that would survive a regulatory exam
  └── Making compliance fast without making it weak
```

### [CommGuard](https://github.com/imansipathak/CommGuard) — AI Communications Governance Portal

Enterprise governance platform for managing the full lifecycle of **regulated customer-facing servicing communications** at a financial institution.

**The problem it solves:** Financial institutions send millions of communications per year — payment reminders, fraud alerts, rate change notices — each subject to Reg Z, TCPA, UDAAP, FDCPA, and 8 other federal regulations. A single non-compliant communication can trigger consent orders or consumer harm. CommGuard is the governance layer between content creation and the send platform.

**Under the hood:**

| Layer | What it does |
|---|---|
| **AI Compliance Scanner** | Claude AI acts as an OCC examiner — scores 0–100, cites exact violations with section references and rewrite suggestions |
| **171-Rule Engine** | Hand-authored rules across 12 regulations: Reg Z · TCPA · CAN-SPAM · UDAAP · GLBA · CARD Act · E-Sign · Reg E · ECOA · FDCPA · SCRA · CCPA |
| **Dynamic Approval Chains** | Compliance → Brand + Legal + Risk (parallel) → Final Approver — generated based on comm type and regulatory exposure |
| **Content Generator** | Drafts compliant communications from scratch — channel-specific constraints baked in (SMS ≤160 chars, email footer requirements) |
| **A/B Testing Engine** | Z-test for proportions with confidence levels; statistical significance at \|Z\| ≥ 1.96 |
| **Regulatory Impact Analyzer** | Given a rule change, identifies every published communication that needs updating |
| **Audit Trail** | Immutable log for every action — regulatory exam package exportable |

**Built with:** Next.js 15 · TypeScript · SQLite · Anthropic Claude · shadcn/ui · Tailwind CSS v4 · Recharts

## How I Think About AI Products

Most AI product work today is about raw capability — can the model do this thing? My focus is a layer above that:

> **Can this model do this thing reliably enough to satisfy a regulator, an auditor, or a compliance officer who will be held personally liable if it goes wrong?**

That question changes everything about how you design the product.

- You don't just need a good prompt. You need **fallbacks** when the AI hallucinates a rule that doesn't exist.
- You don't just need output. You need an **immutable audit trail** that proves what the AI said, when, and why it was accepted.
- You don't just need automation. You need **human-in-the-loop checkpoints** at the exact moments where regulatory exposure is highest.
- You don't just need accuracy. You need **explainability** at the citation level — which section of which regulation, exactly quoted.

This is the PM muscle I've built. Not just shipping AI features. Shipping AI features that survive contact with the real world.
